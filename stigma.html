Stigma Against
Mentally Ill People

def. Stigma.

"a mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance, quality, or person."

What is Stigma?

Simply put, stigma is the ill feeling harboured towards a person that is different, such as a person with a mental illness. Stigma shines a unipositive light towards the mental illness, thus dehumanising the person with the illness. This allows for the mental illness to worsen, putting the victim in an even worse, compared to before.

Why Does it Exist?

Stigma is just like racism; that is, it exists because people are afraid of anything that is different from them. Furthermore, it allows normal people to be at a "superior" level compared to the people that have a mental illness. Also, it gives normal people a sense that they are always "correct" and worth more to society. Unfortunately, stigma, just like racism cannot be wiped out with ease, as such ideas are very normal to feel towards a different person. Only through education can these ideas become extinct.

What Can We Do At A Local Level?

From a young age, children at schools need to be educated against stigma. Things such as "What Stigma Is", "Why Stigma is Bad" and "How to Help". Furthermore, there needs to be centres where social workers can help people suffering from mental illnesses get over the stigma, as often that could be the more difficult thing to get over.
