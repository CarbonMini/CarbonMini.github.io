What Is a
Mental Illness?

def. Mental Illness.

"a condition which causes serious disorder in a person's behaviour or thinking."

With mental illnesses becoming such a prevalent part of our everyday lives, it is difficult not to understand what it means. Put simply, it is a condition which “causes serious disorder in a person’s behaviour or thinking.” And with over 25% of Australians struggling with such illnesses, it is difficult to not have seen the far-reaching effects that it can have on the victim’s personal life, at school or even at work.

There are many different types of mental illnesses that can affect everyone, at any point in their life. The most common ones are ADHD, depression and anxiety, which all are illnesses that can occur at some points throughout one’s life (Excluding ADHD). When people get older, they are not free from mental illnesses either. Currently untreatable mental illnesses such as Parkinson’s or schizophrenia is common throughout the elderly, due to the slow, but unstoppable degradation of the brain.

Every single mental illness causes serious disorder in a person’s behaviour or thinking. They compromise the brains ability to process data efficiently, and often leaves the victim in an “emotionally wrecked state”, which also happens to prevent the person from functioning normally in normal day-to-day tasks. Some people affected by mental illnesses see the only way out of such ailment is suicide due to the negative ideas harboured against the victim; or namely, stigma. However, there are many organisations that are ready to help, as long as the victim is willing to share the experiences with certified social workers.
