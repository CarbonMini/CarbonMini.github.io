Trends of
Mental Illnesses

def. Trends.

"a general direction in which something is developing or changing."

As shown in the image above, the general trend in the cases of mental illness has increased over the past few years. Other than the age groups 4-17, and 16/18-24, Australia has seen a slow increase in the number of cases of mental issues that were reported.

In general, around 1/3 women had a mental health disorder, while comparatively, 1/4 males had a mental health disorder. Furthermore, the top 3 issues of concern to young people, which directly led to their mental health disorder, were things such as "school or study problems", "coping with stress", and "body image".

Mental disorders don't only affect the victim. In other words, mental disorders affect the economy of Australia as a whole; that is, the time taken off due to mental illnesses account for $3.27 billion dollars lost in productivity. Furthermore, 9 million working days are lost every single year, due to the people that have a mental illness.

TThere are many reasons or factors that can be held accountable for the trends that can be observed. Firstly, with mental health issues coming into the spotlight in the recent years, people that had mental health issues that did not know that they had, could have realised that they had a mental disorder. Secondly, with work hours becoming comparatively longer compared to 20 years ago, the stress that is loaded onto the workers allows for a mental disorder to occur. Finally, with technology and social media becoming so prevalent in our everyday lives, pictures of aesthetically pleasing people dominate our feed. The desire to become as aesthetically pleasing, or to be successful as them may cause a mental disorder.
